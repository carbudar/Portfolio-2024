Time Keeper

P5.js (2023)


Conventional clocks mark seconds, minutes, and hours according to a 24 hour cycle. This segmented, discrete, and regimented approach to parsing time is not universal in terms of capturing and measuring of experience.

Everytime I am working on something, time has never felt constant. At times, it would fly by in a blink of an eye. At some other times, I would feel like I am frozen in time. I also found myself checking the numbers on the clock constantly.
With this time-keeper clock experimentation, I visualize the difference in the speed of time that I usually experience. The animation also helps me experience time as faster or slower whenever I need it to. In a way, I hope that this design could act as an instrument to a psycological time control.

Design Process

I designed a few different concepts of time-keeping and explored various possibilites in conversing time unorthodoxly. I ended up with my second sketch as it resembles the planet rotation which is also a way of telling time. One part of the p5.js sketch is at a slower pace with more calming color, while the other is at a faster pace and a louder color. I wanted to convey the relation between the speed of time and emotion. Ideation and Sketches

Features:

Dial when mouse is on the left side of the screen:
Slower paced
Orbit full rotation: 30 seconds
Color change in ring: 5 minutes
Background color change: 3 minutes

Dial when mouse is on the right side of the screen:
Faster paced
Orbit full rotation: 5 seconds
Color change in ring: 15 seconds
Background color change: 1 minute

Reflection

All in all, I think the whole design process sparked more of my interest on creating timed animation in p5.js. I was able to learn more techniques to accomodate my ideas. Though I sometimes came across some bugs in my code, I was able to fix it by learning my code more thoroughly.
