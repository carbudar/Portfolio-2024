Puppy Vision
In the summer, I adopted a little puppy named Charli. By spending plenty of time training and trying to understand her, I came across a study which mentioned that dogs are only able to see yellow and blue. This made me realized how Charli is mostly intrigued by her blue blankets and toys which made me feel obligated to get her all blue everything. The main purpose of this iteration is to help me put myself in Charli's shoes and see colors the way she sees it. This way, I can sort of pick and choose objects that looks interesting accoding to her vision.

Graphic Visualization
Lastly, when exploring the possibilities of this createCapture() feature, I was inspired by the statics in the example starter code from the previous class activity. Design Process

I started the design process by sketching the ideas out. Since this camera is color-based, there were not much things that I added to the sketch. I then tried to write a code for it in p5js by adjusting the pixels. Once that was done, I thought of exploring ascii characters, following a tutorial which I found on youtube. At first, I had a few different characters with varying shapes and sizes that makes up the image, however I did not really like how that looked as it seems too messy. I decided to change it to just one character (@) since it is a very dense character, making the video look more full and neat. I resized the character size to distort the image enough, but not too much until the image is unrecognizable. I also used arrays and forloops to create the repitition of the character 'tiles'.

With this camera, I expect to be able to see similarly to how Charli sees her world and try to accomodate her with that. I tried showing some pictures of her favorite cartoons and toys, which surprisingly the colors looks very similar to how it looks like to the human eye. In simple, I want this camera to be sort of an artistic translator between dogs and humans to put us in the same page when interacting with toys or colors by showing it toys and objects as the main camera subject to read the colors based on puppy's vision.
